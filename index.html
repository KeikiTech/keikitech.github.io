KeikiTech

Teaching Tomorrow's Tech Experts

CS Education for Kids on the Big Island

KeikiTech is a non-profit organization based in Hilo, Hawai'i. We teach kids computer science, software engineering and general tech skills.

Theodore DeRego

Director

Teddy is a Junior in the UHH Computer Science program, and he loves to code. Most of his experience is from game development as a hobby in high school, but now he primarily develops Bioinformatics software at PBARC (which sounds a lot fancier than it really is). Software development can be a fun hobby and is a great career path since it’s a growing industry, so he thinks all kids who are interested in the nuts and bolts of computers should have the opportunity to learn.


Denby Erece

Outreach Coordinator

Denby is a graduate of UH Hilo's Biology program. She's also the mother of a certified hacker-in-training, and enjoys doing anything she can to encourage her kids and their friends in positive educational pursuits -- especially fun ones.


Brian Hall

Executive Director, Instructor

Brian is a Master’s student in the ICS program at UH Manoa, and he works as Research Support at the USDA-PBARC here in Hilo. Before moving to Hawai’i he was an Assistant Director at a Montessori kindergarten in South Korea. He looks forward to an old age surrounded by the amazing technology our students will build.


Mike Purvis

Director of Technology

Mike graduated from UH Hilo and likes to keep active within the growing tech community on Big Island. He's involved in some great Meetup groups forming in the Hilo area, and he volunteers with Hawaii Techworks as well. He thinks it’s a great time to learn programming, and would love to help kids learn in a fun manner. He lives in Hilo and works for an Oahu company, Upspring Media, where he does a lot of Drupal websites and general web/app development.
